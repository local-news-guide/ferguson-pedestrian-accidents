Pedestrian Accidents in Cullman County

Alabama's Pedestrians Deserve Better Protection

Cullman County's growing communities bring more traffic, more construction, and more risk for pedestrians. From the busy intersections of downtown Cullman to the rural highway shoulders connecting smaller towns like Hanceville and Good Hope, pedestrians face dangers that drivers too often ignore.

At Ferguson & Ferguson, we represent pedestrians injured by distracted drivers, speeding vehicles, and negligent property owners across every city and town in Cullman County. Alabama law entitles injured pedestrians to compensation for medical bills, lost wages, pain and suffering, and more.

Alabama is a contributory negligence state. Insurance companies will try to pin even 1% of blame on you to deny your entire claim. You need attorneys who know how to defeat this defense. That's what we do.

Serving All of Cullman County

Pedestrian Accident Attorneys Across the I-65 & US-31 Corridor

Ferguson & Ferguson represents pedestrian accident victims in every community across Cullman County — from the county seat in Cullman to the college campus corridors of Hanceville, from growing suburbs like Good Hope to small highway towns like Baileyton and Fairview.

The I-65 and US-31 corridors connect Cullman County's communities but also bring high-speed traffic through residential areas without adequate pedestrian infrastructure. Towns like South Vinemont and Garden City sit directly on US-31, where pedestrians share narrow shoulders with vehicles traveling 55+ mph. Highway 69 carries traffic through Good Hope, Baileyton, and Fairview with few sidewalks or crosswalks to protect people on foot.

Rural communities like Berlin, Colony, Holly Pond, Dodge City, and West Point face their own dangers — narrow county roads with no shoulders, blind curves, and drivers who don't expect pedestrians. When negligent drivers strike pedestrians anywhere in Cullman County, Ferguson & Ferguson holds them accountable.
