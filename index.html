Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

What does it cost to hire Ferguson & Ferguson?

For personal injury cases, we work on a contingency fee basis — you pay nothing unless we win. For criminal defense, bankruptcy, and domestic relations, we offer free initial consultations and competitive fee structures.

What is Alabama’s contributory negligence rule?

Alabama is one of only a few states that follows contributory negligence. If you are found even 1% at fault, you could be barred from recovering compensation. Insurance companies exploit this aggressively — experienced Alabama attorneys are critical to defeating this defense.

Do you handle cases in both Huntsville and Decatur?

Yes. We have offices in both Huntsville (303 Williams Avenue SW, Suite 321) and Decatur (211 Oak Street NE). We serve clients across all of North Alabama including Madison County, Morgan County, Limestone County, and Cullman County.

Will I work directly with an attorney?

Absolutely. At Ferguson & Ferguson, one attorney handles your case from beginning to end. You will always have direct access to your attorney — not a paralegal or associate. That’s our commitment to personal attention.

How long do I have to file a personal injury claim in Alabama?

The statute of limitations for most personal injury claims in Alabama is two years from the date of injury. For wrongful death, it is two years from the date of death. Critical evidence can disappear quickly, so contact an attorney as soon as possible.

What should I do right after an accident?

Seek medical attention immediately. Call 911 and file a police report. Document the scene with photos. Exchange information with other parties. Do not admit fault or give recorded statements to insurance companies. Then contact an attorney before speaking with any insurer.
