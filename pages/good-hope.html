Good Hope Pedestrian Accidents

Growth That's Outpacing Pedestrian Safety

With a population approaching 2,500 and steady residential growth, Good Hope is evolving from a quiet rural community into a bustling suburb of Cullman. But the infrastructure hasn't kept pace. Highway 69 carries high-speed traffic straight through town, past homes, churches, and Good Hope High School — with almost no sidewalks separating pedestrians from vehicles.

Residents who walk along road shoulders, children heading to school, and families crossing the highway to reach businesses all face dangers that better planning could have prevented. When a negligent driver strikes a pedestrian on Highway 69 or Good Hope's residential roads, the injuries are often catastrophic.

Alabama Pedestrian Law

Know Your Rights as a Pedestrian in Alabama

⚖ Key Alabama Pedestrian Laws

  • Drivers must yield to pedestrians in marked crosswalks (Ala. Code § 32-5A-210)
  • Pedestrians have the right-of-way at signalized intersections when the walk signal is active
  • Alabama follows contributory negligence — if you're found even 1% at fault, you could lose your entire claim
  • Drivers must exercise due care to avoid striking pedestrians on any roadway (Ala. Code § 32-5A-214)
  • Hit-and-run drivers face criminal charges under Alabama law, and you may still recover through uninsured motorist coverage
  • The statute of limitations for personal injury in Alabama is 2 years from the date of the accident

No sidewalk doesn't mean no rights. Insurance companies will argue you shouldn't have been walking along Highway 69 when there's no sidewalk. Alabama law requires drivers to exercise due care regardless. Ferguson & Ferguson knows how to defeat these bad-faith arguments.

After an Accident

What to Do After a Pedestrian Accident in Good Hope

  1. Call 911 Immediately

    Get medical help and create an official police report. Good Hope Police or Cullman County Sheriff will respond and document the scene — this is critical evidence.

  2. Seek Medical Attention

    Even if you feel okay, get checked at Cullman Regional Medical Center. Pedestrian impact injuries often don't show symptoms for hours or days.

  3. Document Everything

    Photograph the scene, your injuries, the vehicle, road conditions, and the lack of sidewalks or crosswalks. Get witness contact information before they leave.

  4. Do Not Give Recorded Statements

    The driver's insurance company will call quickly. They're looking for statements to use against you. Politely decline and refer them to your attorney.
