Legal Framework

Alabama Pedestrian Accident Law

Contributory Negligence Rule

Alabama follows a strict contributory negligence standard. If you are found even 1% at fault for your injuries, you may be barred from recovering compensation. Insurance companies exploit this rule aggressively, blaming pedestrians for wearing dark clothes, crossing mid-block, or stepping into traffic.

We fight back. Our attorneys investigate thoroughly, gathering evidence that proves the driver's negligence was the sole cause of your injuries. We don't let insurers twist Alabama law to deny you justice.

Protecting Your Rights

What To Do After a Pedestrian Accident in Good Hope

The steps you take immediately after an accident can make or break your claim.

1

Get Medical Attention

Call 911 and request an ambulance. Even if you feel okay, adrenaline can mask serious injuries. A prompt medical evaluation creates documentation linking your injuries to the accident.

2

Report the Accident

Ensure the Good Hope Police Department files an official report. This document becomes critical evidence. If you're too injured to speak, have a witness or family member ensure it's done.

3

Document Everything

If possible, photograph the accident scene, your injuries, road conditions, lack of sidewalks, and any traffic signs. Get contact information from witnesses who saw what happened.

4

Don't Talk to Insurance

The driver's insurance company will call quickly, sounding sympathetic. They're building a case against you. Do not give a recorded statement or sign anything without legal counsel.
