Alabama Law & Your Pedestrian Accident Claim

Contributory Negligence

Alabama follows a contributory negligence rule. If you are found even 1% at fault, you may be barred from recovery. Insurance companies will try to blame you for "jaywalking" or "not paying attention." We gather evidence to prove the driver's fault and protect your right to compensation.

What to Do After a Pedestrian Accident in Fairview

1

Get Medical Attention

Call 911 immediately. Even if injuries seem minor, internal trauma and head injuries can worsen without treatment.

2

Report the Accident

File a report with Fairview Police or the Cullman County Sheriff's Office. An official report documents the crash and the driver's information.

3

Document the Scene

Take photos of the roadway, your injuries, vehicle damage, and any skid marks or debris. Collect witness names and contact information.

4

Preserve Evidence

Keep all medical records, hospital bills, prescription receipts, and clothing you were wearing. Do not post about the accident on social media.

5

Call a Lawyer Before Talking to Insurance

Insurance adjusters will try to minimize your claim or blame you for the accident. Contact Ferguson & Ferguson before giving any recorded statements.
