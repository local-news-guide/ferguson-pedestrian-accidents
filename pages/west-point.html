West Point Pedestrian Accidents

Dangerous Crossroads in a Rural Community

West Point is a small Cullman County community of about 500 people situated where several county roads converge. These intersections — governed by stop signs rather than traffic signals — create particular hazards for pedestrians. Turning vehicles focus on merging with cross-traffic and frequently fail to check for people on foot.

Agricultural activity adds another dimension. Farm equipment approaching intersections blocks visibility for both pedestrians and drivers. Tractors making wide turns at crossroads can push pedestrians off the road or into the path of oncoming vehicles.

Ferguson & Ferguson represents pedestrians injured at West Point's intersections and along its county roads. We understand how uncontrolled crossroads, agricultural traffic, and poor sight lines combine to create deadly conditions.

Common Causes

Why Pedestrian Accidents Happen in West Point

  • 1

    Uncontrolled Intersection Dangers

    West Point's intersections rely on stop signs rather than traffic signals. Drivers rolling through stops strike pedestrians crossing at these intersections.

  • 2

    Turning Vehicles Ignoring Pedestrians

    Drivers making turns at county road intersections focus on vehicle traffic and fail to scan for pedestrians in or near the crossroad.

  • 3

    Poor Sight Lines at Crossroads

    Vegetation, terrain, and parked farm equipment block drivers' views of approaching pedestrians until the last moment.

  • 4

    Agricultural Vehicle Conflicts

    Tractors and farm equipment making wide turns at intersections force pedestrians off the road or into traffic lanes.

  • 5

    Speeding Between Intersections

    Drivers accelerate aggressively between stop-sign intersections, reaching unsafe speeds on short stretches where pedestrians may be walking.

  • 6

    No Pedestrian Crossings

    West Point has no marked crosswalks, pedestrian signals, or crossing infrastructure of any kind. Pedestrians cross entirely at their own judgment.

Alabama Pedestrian Law

Know Your Rights as a Pedestrian in Alabama

⚖ Key Alabama Pedestrian Laws

  • Drivers must yield to pedestrians in marked crosswalks (Ala. Code § 32-5A-210)
  • Pedestrians have the right-of-way at signalized intersections when the walk signal is active
  • Alabama follows contributory negligence — if you are found even 1% at fault, you could lose your entire claim
  • Drivers must exercise due care to avoid striking pedestrians on any roadway (Ala. Code § 32-5A-214)
  • Hit-and-run drivers face criminal charges, and you may still recover through uninsured motorist coverage
  • The statute of limitations for personal injury in Alabama is 2 years from the date of the accident

Stop signs protect vehicles, not pedestrians. Uncontrolled intersections give pedestrians zero protection. Drivers owe a duty of care to watch for people on foot at every intersection. Ferguson & Ferguson holds them to that standard.

After an Accident

What to Do After a Pedestrian Accident in West Point

  1. Call 911 Immediately

    Call 911. The Cullman County Sheriff will respond. Make sure a police report documents the intersection, traffic control, and conditions.

  2. Seek Medical Attention

    Go to Cullman Regional Medical Center. Intersection impacts involve turning vehicles that can cause complex injuries.

  3. Document Everything

    Photograph the intersection, sight lines, stop signs, vegetation blocking views, your injuries, and the vehicle. Document where you were when struck.

  4. Do Not Give Recorded Statements

    The driver's insurance company will call quickly. They want statements to twist against you under Alabama's contributory negligence rule. Politely decline and refer them to your attorney.
