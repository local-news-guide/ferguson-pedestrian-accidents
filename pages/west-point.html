West Point Pedestrian Accidents

Intersection Dangers in a Rural Crossroads Community

West Point sits at the intersection of county roads that serve as lifelines for this community of approximately 500 residents. While these crossroads connect neighbors and provide access to essential services, they also create dangerous crossing points for pedestrians.

Without traffic signals to control vehicle movement, West Point relies entirely on stop signs at key intersections. Drivers who fail to yield, misjudge pedestrian speed, or accelerate too quickly from stops create serious hazards for residents walking to the post office, local stores, or along county roads.

Uncontrolled intersections and turning vehicles at crossroads create unique pedestrian dangers in West Point's rural setting.

Alabama Law

Your Legal Rights After a Pedestrian Accident

Contributory Negligence Rule

Alabama follows contributory negligence (Ala. Code ¬ß 6-5-521). If you are found even 1% at fault for your injuries, you may be barred from recovery. This makes it critical to establish that the driver‚Äînot you‚Äîviolated Alabama traffic laws or failed to exercise reasonable care at the intersection.

Right-of-Way at Intersections

Ala. Code ¬ß 32-5A-72: Drivers approaching stop-sign controlled intersections must yield the right-of-way to pedestrians lawfully crossing within marked or unmarked crosswalks.

Ala. Code ¬ß 32-5A-210: Pedestrians facing traffic control devices must obey those signals, but drivers must still exercise due care to avoid colliding with pedestrians.

Two-Year Statute of Limitations

Ala. Code ¬ß 6-2-38: You have two years from the date of the accident to file a personal injury lawsuit. Missing this deadline typically means losing your right to compensation forever.
