Garden City Pedestrian Accidents

A Growing Town Where Pedestrian Safety Lags Behind

Garden City is experiencing steady growth that transforms a rural community into a suburban one. With a population around 800 and climbing, more families, more vehicles, and more foot traffic share roads designed for a fraction of today's use.

Garden City Elementary School is a particular concern. Twice daily, children and parents navigate roads without adequate crosswalks, crossing guards, or reduced-speed enforcement. Drivers who fail to slow in school zones put the community's most vulnerable members at risk.

Alabama Pedestrian Law

Know Your Rights as a Pedestrian in Alabama

⚖ Key Alabama Pedestrian Laws

  • Drivers must yield to pedestrians in marked crosswalks (Ala. Code § 32-5A-210)
  • Pedestrians have the right-of-way at signalized intersections when the walk signal is active
  • Alabama follows contributory negligence — if you are found even 1% at fault, you could lose your entire claim
  • Drivers must exercise due care to avoid striking pedestrians on any roadway (Ala. Code § 32-5A-214)
  • Hit-and-run drivers face criminal charges, and you may still recover through uninsured motorist coverage
  • The statute of limitations for personal injury in Alabama is 2 years from the date of the accident

Children deserve better protection. When a driver strikes a child in a school zone or on a residential road, the consequences are devastating. Ferguson & Ferguson holds negligent drivers fully accountable.

After an Accident

What to Do After a Pedestrian Accident in Garden City

  1. Call 911 Immediately

    Call 911. Garden City Police or Cullman County Sheriff will respond. If a child is involved, request EMS immediately regardless of apparent injury severity.

  2. Seek Medical Attention

    Take your child or yourself to Cullman Regional Medical Center. Children are especially vulnerable to head injuries and internal trauma from pedestrian impacts.

  3. Document Everything

    Photograph the scene, the vehicle, injuries, road conditions, school zone signage, and any lack of crosswalks. Get contact information from witnesses.

  4. Do Not Give Recorded Statements

    The driver's insurance company will call quickly. They want statements to twist against you under Alabama's contributory negligence rule. Politely decline and refer them to your attorney.
