Alabama Pedestrian Law

Legal Standards for Garden City Cases

Contributory Negligence Rule

Alabama follows a strict contributory negligence standard. If a pedestrian is found even 1% at fault for their own injury, they cannot recover compensation. Insurance companies exploit this rule by blaming pedestrians for driver negligence.

Ferguson & Ferguson counters these tactics by thoroughly investigating accident scenes, securing witness statements, and reconstructing events to prove driver fault and pedestrian compliance with traffic laws.

Key Alabama Code Sections

  • Ala. Code ¬ß 32-5A-210: Right-of-way in crosswalks
  • Ala. Code ¬ß 32-5A-220: Pedestrian control signals
  • Ala. Code ¬ß 32-5A-221: Pedestrian right-of-way
  • Ala. Code ¬ß 32-5A-40: School zone speed limits
  • Ala. Code ¬ß 6-2-38: Two-year statute of limitations for injury claims

Garden City pedestrian cases require immediate investigation to preserve evidence and protect your right to compensation. The two-year deadline starts from your injury date.

After a Pedestrian Accident

What To Do in Garden City

  1. Call 911 Immediately

    Get medical attention and create an official police report. Garden City PD or Cullman County Sheriff will document the scene and driver information.

  2. Document the Scene

    If able, photograph vehicle position, road conditions, lack of crosswalks/sidewalks, traffic signs, school zone markings, and your injuries. Collect witness contact information.

  3. Seek Medical Treatment

    Go to Cullman Regional Medical Center or your doctor immediately. Document all injuries, even if they seem minor. Delayed treatment weakens your case.

  4. Do Not Give Recorded Statements

    Insurance adjusters will contact you quickly. Politely decline to give recorded statements or sign documents without attorney review. They will use your words against you.
