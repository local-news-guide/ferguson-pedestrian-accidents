Alabama Pedestrian Law

Know Your Rights as a Pedestrian in Alabama

⚖ Key Alabama Pedestrian Laws

  • Drivers must yield to pedestrians in marked crosswalks (Ala. Code § 32-5A-210)
  • Pedestrians have the right-of-way at signalized intersections when the walk signal is active
  • Alabama follows contributory negligence — if you're found even 1% at fault, you could lose your entire claim
  • Drivers must exercise due care to avoid striking pedestrians on any roadway (Ala. Code § 32-5A-214)
  • Hit-and-run drivers face criminal charges under Alabama law, and you may still recover through uninsured motorist coverage
  • The statute of limitations for personal injury in Alabama is 2 years from the date of the accident

Insurance companies target college students. They'll argue you were distracted by your phone, wearing earbuds, or jaywalking between classes. Ferguson & Ferguson knows how to dismantle these arguments and protect your claim under Alabama's harsh contributory negligence standard.

After an Accident

What to Do After a Pedestrian Accident in Hanceville

  1. Call 911 Immediately

    Get medical help and create an official police report. Hanceville Police Department will respond and document the scene — this report is critical evidence for your case.

  2. Get Medical Treatment

    Go to Cullman Regional Medical Center or your doctor immediately. Pedestrian impact injuries — head trauma, internal bleeding, spinal injuries — often don't show symptoms for hours. Early documentation links your injuries to the accident.

  3. Document the Scene

    Photograph the location, your injuries, the vehicle, traffic signals, and any lack of crosswalks or sidewalks. Near Wallace State, capture the campus context — parking lot configurations, pedestrian traffic patterns, and lighting conditions.

  4. Do Not Give Recorded Statements

    The driver's insurance company will call quickly. They want statements they can use against you under contributory negligence. Politely decline and refer them to your attorney.
