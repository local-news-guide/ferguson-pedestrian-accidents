Dodge City Pedestrian Accidents

New Development, Missing Safety Infrastructure

Dodge City is one of Cullman County's emerging growth areas, with new homes and subdivisions drawing families to the community. But residential development has far outpaced pedestrian safety planning. New neighborhoods connect to town via roads with no sidewalks, no streetlights, and no safe crossings.

Construction vehicles sharing residential roads with pedestrians add another layer of danger. Dump trucks, concrete mixers, and heavy equipment operate alongside families walking to neighbors' homes and children playing near the road.

Alabama Pedestrian Law

Know Your Rights as a Pedestrian in Alabama

⚖ Key Alabama Pedestrian Laws

  • Drivers must yield to pedestrians in marked crosswalks (Ala. Code § 32-5A-210)
  • Pedestrians have the right-of-way at signalized intersections when the walk signal is active
  • Alabama follows contributory negligence — if you are found even 1% at fault, you could lose your entire claim
  • Drivers must exercise due care to avoid striking pedestrians on any roadway (Ala. Code § 32-5A-214)
  • Hit-and-run drivers face criminal charges, and you may still recover through uninsured motorist coverage
  • The statute of limitations for personal injury in Alabama is 2 years from the date of the accident

Developers and drivers share responsibility. When neighborhoods are built without sidewalks and a driver strikes a pedestrian, both the infrastructure failure and the driver's negligence may be at play. Ferguson & Ferguson investigates all angles.

After an Accident

What to Do After a Pedestrian Accident in Dodge City

  1. Call 911 Immediately

    Call 911. Cullman County Sheriff and EMS will respond. Ensure a police report is filed documenting road conditions and lack of pedestrian infrastructure.

  2. Seek Medical Attention

    Go to Cullman Regional Medical Center even if injuries seem minor. Pedestrian impacts — even at lower residential speeds — cause serious internal injuries.

  3. Document Everything

    Photograph the road, lack of sidewalks and lighting, construction activity, your injuries, and the vehicle. Note any construction company names on vehicles or signs.

  4. Do Not Give Recorded Statements

    The driver's insurance company will call quickly. They want statements to twist against you under Alabama's contributory negligence rule. Politely decline and refer them to your attorney.
