Colony Pedestrian Accidents

A Quiet Community Where Shared Roads Create Danger

Colony sits along County Road 222 in Cullman County — a tight-knit community of roughly 350 people where walking is simply part of daily life. Residents walk between neighbors' homes, jog along the road for exercise, and walk their dogs on the same roadways used by vehicles traveling far too fast.

With no sidewalks, no crosswalks, and no traffic controls, Colony's roads offer zero protection for pedestrians. Community events and church gatherings periodically create clusters of people on foot, but drivers passing through rarely slow down.

Ferguson & Ferguson represents pedestrians struck on Colony's county roads. We understand that small-town walking culture is not negligence — and we hold drivers accountable when their carelessness injures people in this community.

Alabama Pedestrian Law

Know Your Rights as a Pedestrian in Alabama

⚖ Key Alabama Pedestrian Laws

  • Drivers must yield to pedestrians in marked crosswalks (Ala. Code § 32-5A-210)
  • Pedestrians have the right-of-way at signalized intersections when the walk signal is active
  • Alabama follows contributory negligence — if you are found even 1% at fault, you could lose your entire claim
  • Drivers must exercise due care to avoid striking pedestrians on any roadway (Ala. Code § 32-5A-214)
  • Hit-and-run drivers face criminal charges, and you may still recover through uninsured motorist coverage
  • The statute of limitations for personal injury in Alabama is 2 years from the date of the accident

Small-town walking is not carelessness. Insurance companies will argue you shouldn't have been walking on a road without sidewalks. Ferguson & Ferguson fights back by proving the driver's negligence was the sole cause of your injuries.

After an Accident

What to Do After a Pedestrian Accident in Colony

  1. Call 911 Immediately

    Call 911. The Cullman County Sheriff and county EMS will respond. An official police report documenting the scene is essential evidence.

  2. Seek Medical Attention

    Even if you feel okay, go to Cullman Regional Medical Center. Pedestrian impacts cause internal injuries that may not show symptoms for hours.

  3. Document Everything

    Photograph the road, lack of shoulders, blind curves, lighting conditions, your injuries, and the vehicle. Get witness contact info from neighbors who saw the accident.

  4. Do Not Give Recorded Statements

    The driver's insurance company will call quickly. They want statements to twist against you under Alabama's contributory negligence rule. Politely decline and refer them to your attorney.
