Understanding Your Rights

Alabama Pedestrian Accident Law

Alabama is a contributory negligence state. If you are found even 1% at fault for the accident, you may be barred from recovering any compensation. Insurance companies exploit this rule to deny valid claims.

Under Alabama law:

  • Drivers must yield to pedestrians in crosswalks (Ala. Code ¬ß 32-5A-211)
  • Pedestrians must use sidewalks where available and cross at marked intersections when possible (Ala. Code ¬ß 32-5A-215)
  • You have two years from the date of injury to file a personal injury lawsuit (Ala. Code ¬ß 6-2-38)

In Colony, where no sidewalks or crosswalks exist, we build evidence showing the driver's duty to watch for pedestrians on rural roads‚Äîand their failure to do so.

Take Action Now

What to Do After a Pedestrian Accident in Colony

  1. Call 911 Immediately

    Contact the Cullman County Sheriff's Office and request EMS. Even if injuries seem minor, internal trauma and head injuries may not be immediately apparent. A police report documents the scene and driver statements.

  2. Get Medical Attention

    Rural EMS response times can vary. If transported, go to Cullman Regional Medical Center or request a trauma evaluation. Document all injuries, treatments, and follow-up care.

  3. Preserve Evidence

    Take photos of the scene, road conditions, lack of sidewalks or lighting, vehicle damage, your injuries, and any torn clothing or personal items. Get contact information from witnesses.

  4. Do Not Give Statements to Insurance

    The driver's insurance company will contact you quickly. Do not provide a recorded statement, sign releases, or accept early settlement offers without legal counsel.

    We offer free consultations. Our team investigates the crash, gathers evidence, consults accident reconstruction experts, and fights for compensation covering medical bills, lost wages, pain and suffering, and permanent disability.
