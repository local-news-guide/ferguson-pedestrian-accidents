Your Rights Under Alabama Law

What You Need to Know

Contributory Negligence & the Pure Bar Rule

Alabama follows a strict contributory negligence standard. If you are found even 1% at fault for the accident, you may be barred from recovering any compensation. This makes it critical to have an experienced attorney who can counter defense arguments that you were jaywalking, wearing dark clothing, or otherwise contributed to the collision.

Protect Your Claim

What to Do After a Pedestrian Accident in South Vinemont

  1. Get Medical Help Immediately

    Call 911 or have someone drive you to the nearest emergency room. Hidden injuries like internal bleeding or traumatic brain injury can be fatal if untreated. Prompt medical records also establish causation.

  2. Report the Crash to Police

    An official accident report documents the driver's insurance, vehicle details, and initial statements. Request a copy for your attorney.

  3. Document the Scene (If Possible)

    Take photos of the roadway, lighting conditions, lack of sidewalks, and any visible injuries. Note the time of day and weather. If it was nighttime, photograph the darkness.

  4. Preserve Clothing and Evidence

    Keep the clothes you were wearing‚Äîthey may show impact patterns or refute claims that you were invisible. Do not wash or discard them.

  5. Contact Ferguson & Ferguson Before Giving Statements

    Insurance adjusters will try to get you to say you "stepped out into traffic" or "should have worn reflective clothing." Let us handle all communication. Your free consultation is confidential and costs nothing unless we win.
