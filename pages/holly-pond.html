Highway 31 Danger

Pedestrian Safety in a Small Town on a Major Highway

Holly Pond sits along Highway 31, a high-speed corridor that carries heavy through traffic between Cullman and points north. For a town of 800 residents, that creates a dangerous paradox: a walkable, close-knit community intersected by a road where drivers routinely exceed 55 mph.

Add school zones near Holly Pond High School, limited crosswalks through the town center, and pedestrians forced onto narrow shoulders, and you have a recipe for serious accidents. When those accidents happen, victims face catastrophic injuries—broken bones, traumatic brain injuries, spinal damage, or worse.

Ferguson & Ferguson represents Holly Pond pedestrians injured by negligent drivers. We investigate the crash, identify all liable parties, and fight for full compensation.

6 Common Causes of Pedestrian Accidents in Holly Pond

1

Highway 31 Speeds

Drivers treating Highway 31 through Holly Pond like an open highway, ignoring reduced speed zones and striking pedestrians crossing or walking along the road.

2

School Zone Violations

Drivers blowing through school zones near Holly Pond High School during drop-off or dismissal, endangering students on foot or bike.

3

Pedestrians on Highway Shoulders

Walkers forced onto narrow or nonexistent shoulders along Highway 31, struck by inattentive or distracted drivers who drift off the roadway.

4

Limited Crosswalks

Lack of marked crossings through Holly Pond's town center, leading pedestrians to cross mid-block or at unmarked intersections where drivers don't expect them.

5

Nighttime Hazards

Poor lighting along Highway 31 and side streets, making pedestrians nearly invisible after dark—especially those wearing dark clothing or crossing without reflective gear.

6

Intersection Dangers

Drivers failing to yield to pedestrians in crosswalks at Highway 31 intersections, or turning right-on-red without checking for walkers.

Alabama Pedestrian Accident Law

Contributory Negligence Rule

Alabama follows a harsh contributory negligence rule: if you are found even 1% at fault for the accident, you cannot recover damages. Insurance companies exploit this by claiming the pedestrian "darted out" or was "jaywalking." We gather evidence—witness statements, surveillance video, accident reconstructions—to prove the driver was 100% at fault.

Key Alabama Code Sections

  • Ala. Code § 32-5A-220: Drivers must yield to pedestrians in crosswalks.
  • Ala. Code § 32-5A-221: Drivers must exercise due care to avoid striking pedestrians.
  • Ala. Code § 32-5A-215: Pedestrians must use crosswalks where provided, but drivers still owe a duty of care even when pedestrians cross outside marked areas.
Two-Year Statute of Limitations: You have two years from the date of the accident to file a personal injury lawsuit in Alabama. Evidence degrades and witnesses' memories fade—contact Ferguson & Ferguson immediately to protect your claim.

What To Do After a Pedestrian Accident in Holly Pond

1. Get Medical Help

Call 911 immediately. Even if you feel "okay," adrenaline can mask serious injuries. Request transport to Cullman Regional Medical Center or another equipped facility. Internal injuries, head trauma, and fractures may not show symptoms for hours.

2. Report to Holly Pond VFD or Cullman County Sheriff

If you're able, ensure law enforcement responds and files a crash report. Holly Pond Volunteer Fire Department often assists at accident scenes, and Cullman County Sheriff's Office has jurisdiction. Get the report number and officer's name.

3. Document Everything

Take photos of your injuries, the crash scene, vehicle damage, skid marks, and road conditions. Get contact information from witnesses. If the driver flees, note the vehicle's make, model, color, and license plate if possible.

4. Preserve Evidence

Keep the clothing and shoes you were wearing. Take photos of bruises and wounds as they develop over the next few days. Save all medical records, bills, and prescription receipts.
